Banga Soup (Urhobo Style)

If you're looking for a truly rich and unforgettable Nigerian dish, Banga Soup is the one.It's a palm fruit delicacy from the Niger Delta region.It hits with a bold flavour and deep native spicy aroma that will have you coming for more.

Recipe Details

  • Prep Time: 20 minutes
  • Cook Time: 1 hour
  • Servings: 5-6 people
  • Difficulty: medium

What It Looks Like

Ingredients You'll be Needing

  • Palm fruits (Banga extract)
  • Beef or goat meat
  • Fresh catfish or dread fish
  • Banga spice (Oburunbebe stick, otiako and irugeje)
  • Grounded Crayfish
  • Palm oil (optional, for coloring)
  • Salt and seasoning cubes (Magic)
  • Fresh pepper and onions
  • Fresh scent leaves (or dried if fresh is unavailable)

How to make it

  1. Boil your meat with the spices, onions and seasoning cubes.
  2. Pour your banga extract into a pot and allow it to boil until it begins to thicken.
  3. Add the cooked meat and fish into the boiling Banga base.
  4. Sprinkle in your Banga spices, crayfish, and blended pepper. Stir gently.
  5. Let boil on low heat until the flavours blend together.
  6. Add scent leaves towards the end for that beautiful native aroma.
  7. Allow the soup to rest for a few minutes before serving.

Helpful Tips

If you want the soup to taste like real Delta style Banga Soup, don't skip the traditional spices they're the heart of this meal.

Pro Tips: Serve it with starch, pounded yam, or fufu for the full experience
